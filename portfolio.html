Chief Exicutive Officer Creative Web Design

In the early days of the commercial web, nearly everyone building websites was considered a web designer. At the time, digital experiences were relatively simple, with most websites consisting of a collection of static pages connected through buttons and hyperlinks. Compared to today's interactive applications, these early websites offered straightforward navigation and limited functionality.

As websites became more complex and information-rich, web designers often collaborated with information architects to ensure that content was organized in a way that made it easy for users to navigate, find information, and complete their tasks effectively.
